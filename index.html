Accessibility is a Requirement

Description

As someone who is legally blind without my glasses and used to rely on screen readers to navigate the digital world, I know firsthand how critical accessibility is. The web should be a place where everyone—regardless of ability—can participate fully. Yet, thousands of businesses face lawsuits every year for failing to meet accessibility standards. In 2022 alone, over 3,200 digital accessibility lawsuits were filed in the U.S., a number that continues to rise.

I’m not just an advocate—I’m a developer who makes websites accessible. I specialize in creating inclusive, functional, and compliant digital experiences for the disabled community. Whether it’s ensuring screen reader compatibility, optimizing for keyboard navigation, or designing with colorblind users in mind, I provide tailored solutions that meet both legal requirements and user needs.

If you’re ready to make your website accessible and avoid costly lawsuits, let’s work together. Accessibility isn’t just the right thing to do—it’s a necessity. And I’m here to help you get it done.
